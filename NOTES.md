The model relies on the trailing acoustic and timbral prosody of the final 150ms of speech compared to the preceding 1.5s context window. Instead of using silence duration, it tracks 29 features including relative pitch drops, energy fading, Zero Crossing Rate (ZCR), Spectral Flatness, and Spectral Rolloff. These specific spectral signals measure "harmonicity versus noise," successfully capturing the physiological shift when a human stops vibrating their vocal cords and exhales to yield a turn. The model still fails on abrupt conversational cutoffs, such as a user suddenly stopping due to background noise, being interrupted, or experiencing audio packet loss. It also struggles with highly expressive, non-standard speech where a user utilizes a dramatic pitch drop mid-sentence for rhetorical effect rather than to yield the floor. With one more day, I would abandon manual statistical aggregations (means, standard deviations, and slopes) across fixed time windows. Instead, I would build a recurrent sequence model, specifically a Temporal Convolutional Network (TCN) or a GRU. I would feed the raw frame-by-frame pitch, energy, and MFCC time-series data directly into this network. This would allow the model to autonomously learn the exact temporal curvature and subtle acoustic patterns of turn-yielding across different languages. Finally, I would introduce data augmentation (adding background noise and simulated interruptions) to force the model to become robust against the abrupt cutoffs it currently fails on.
