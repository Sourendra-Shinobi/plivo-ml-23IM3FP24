End-of-Turn (EOT) Detection Speedrun

Final Performance: Mean response delay of ~100 ms at ≤ 5% false cutoffs.
Status: Decisively beat the 1600ms silence-only baseline.

1. Solution Architecture

The core philosophy of this model is that silence is not a feature; trailing prosody is. To prevent interrupting users taking a breath, the model analyzes the final 1.5 seconds of audio prior to a pause. The pipeline extracts a 26-dimensional feature vector per turn:

  • Pitch & Energy Deltas: Measuring the gradient of F0 and volume drops in the final 150ms.
  • Spectral Centroid: Captures the "brightness" of the voice, which naturally drops as humans trail off at the end of a thought.
  • Zero Crossing Rate (ZCR): Spikes when speech trails into unvoiced fricatives or exhales.
  • MFCCs: 5 coefficients (mean and standard deviation) to capture the overall vocal tract shape.

These features are normalized via StandardScaler and classified using a RandomForestClassifier (n_estimators=300, max_depth=12, class_weight="balanced_subsample"). Random Forest was chosen because it cleanly maps the non-linear thresholds of acoustic data without requiring the massive datasets needed for deep learning.

2. Human vs. AI Contribution

Human Reasoning (The "Why")

I defined the acoustic hypothesis: humans drop pitch and lose vocal brightness when yielding the floor. I made the architectural decision to switch from basic volume tracking to librosa spectral features (Centroid/ZCR) to ensure the model generalizes across languages (specifically the hidden Hindi set), as timbral shifts are more universal than language-specific intonation rules.

AI Coding Agent (The "How")

The AI assistant was utilized to quickly write the boilerplate code for the scikit-learn pipeline, handle the NumPy array slicing (calculating the 150ms end-windows), and format the librosa extraction loop to avoid shape mismatch errors on very short audio segments.

3. Summary of Runlog and Notes

The iterative process (detailed in RUNLOG.md) proved that VAD/silence-timers are fundamentally flawed for conversational AI because they cannot differentiate semantic intent. By iterating from basic energy metrics to advanced spectral analysis, we drastically reduced response latency. As noted in NOTES.md, the model's current limitation is abrupt conversational interruptions. Future work would replace this Random Forest with a Temporal Convolutional Network (TCN) to process the raw audio time-series natively.
