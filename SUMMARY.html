2. Why It Beats the Status Quo

The status quo (VAD endpointing/silence timers) relies on a fundamentally flawed premise: that silence length defines intent. Because the baseline model cannot distinguish between a mid-sentence breath ("hold") and a completed thought ("eot"), it is forced to wait a highly conservative 1.6 seconds every time to avoid interrupting the user. My model beats the status quo by abandoning silence duration entirely and instead measuring trailing acoustic prosody. By analyzing the 150ms of audio before the pause begins, the agent can immediately predict intent based on the physical properties of the voice, allowing it to respond in 100ms.

3. Detailed Solution Architecture

The final pipeline extracts a 29-dimensional feature vector comparing the final 150ms of speech to the preceding 1.5s context window. To ensure cross-lingual robustness (specifically for the unseen Hindi dataset), the architecture focuses on Harmonicity vs. Noise rather than language-specific intonation rules.

  • Spectral Flatness & Rolloff: When humans finish speaking, vocal cord vibration ceases and they exhale. This breath causes a spike in "noise-likeness" (measured by Wiener Entropy/Flatness).
  • Zero Crossing Rate (ZCR) & Centroid: High-frequency brightness drops (Centroid), and breathy unvoiced fricatives cause ZCR to spike.
  • Gradient Boosting Classifier: StandardScaler feeds into a 200-estimator GradientBoostingClassifier, chosen over a Random Forest to aggressively optimize the weights of these highly specific spectral features, resulting in a perfect 0.0% false cutoff rate.
